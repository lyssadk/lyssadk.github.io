TARGET AUDIENCE

The target audience is parents who are concerned for safety during the weather (sun, water, etc.), students who want to know what to do during different weathers, active people who like to do things outdoors and need to know what they can do depending on weather condidtions (like bikers and runners), and anyone living in the Idaho areas that want to know the weather so they know how to dress, or what to expect.

2 PERSONAS

Natalie Hansen

Description: A 26 year old mother of a 2 year old and a 3 year old who have tried sneaking into bodies of water and playing outside when it's sunny. She's concerned for their safety because of how energetic and sneaky they are. They cause a lot of mischief together int their terror toddler years. Her husband is normally working all day, meaning for the most part she is alone in watching the two rambunctious boys. She enjoys swimming herself but hasn't found time since she's so busy with her boys and doing her online homework since she's taking some college classes to further her education.

Adam Parker

Description: A 19 year old college athlete who is really interested in anything outdoorsy. He likes to go on hikes, play outdoor sports, and is majoring in Art education. He's not even sure what that entails as it's his first semester, but it allows him to take a photography class, which he has grown interested in recently. He's from Oregon where there is normally decent weather to do hikes and it's been an adjustment adjusting to Idaho's weather. He also has a part-time job at a bbq Restaurant where he makes just over minimum wage, allowing him to pay for almost everything he needs, and his parents provide the difference.

5 SCENARIOS

A concerned parent is anxious about their now walking toddler who seems to find ways to get to the water. They've had a close call already and want to know where to go and what to do for water safety with their child, and come across our website's safety page.

An avid runner has been wondering if the recent rain has ruined the running trails, so they look it up and are guided to our page about outdoor activities and running safety.

A group of friends are wondering what there is to do near them despite the bad weather. They look it up and come across ideas for outdoor activities and safety tips for snowy and rainy weather. It also tells them what to do at home when the weather is too dangerous or unpleasant.

A pair of best friends are looking to go tanning but don't know when is the best and safest time to go sun tanning. They look it up and find our sun safety page where it goes over just that.

A college student has to walk to class and wants to know what the weather is like so they know how warm to dress. Our website pulls up and tells them the weather in their region.
